What is a Benchmark?


A benchmark is a performance test that measures how well your computer hardware—such as the processor (CPU), graphics card (GPU), and memory (RAM)—handles specific tasks, games, or software workloads. Benchmarking allows you to evaluate the strengths and weaknesses of your system, compare different setups, and see how your PC performs against others.
In this section, you’ll find detailed benchmark results from various processors and graphics cards, tested with popular modern games. These results can help you make informed decisions when upgrading or building your next gaming PC. Whether you want to know how many frames per second (FPS) your system can achieve in different titles or simply compare hardware efficiency, our benchmarks provide reliable insights based on real-world testing.
